Polaris      

If you have ever wondered what is the name of the North Star, this is the answer. Without a doubt, it’s one of the most popular stars. It belongs to Ursa Minor, and it’s also famous as the Guiding Star. Polaris shine has been of great importance in designing navigation charts throughout history

Castor

It’s one of the stars that formed the Gemini. Castor is the twin of Pollux. In ancient Greece, it was also known as Apollo, in China – as Yin, one of the fundamental forces that give life balance.
Pollux

This is a name of Greek origin that means kindness and belongs to the other star that formed the Gemini constellation. It’s an orange-red star, the brightest in the Gemini constellation and the seventeenth familiar brightest star.

Orion

It’s one of the most famous constellations for being most visible at plain sight. The Orion’s belt is formed by Mintaka, Alnitak, and Alnilam, forming an alignment between nebulas and clouds of stellar dust.
Sirius

It is the brightest star, located in the Canis Major constellation. It can be appreciated from any part of the planet earth with a naked eye.
Altair

This name has an Arabic origin that means flying. Altair belongs to the Eagle constellation and is the twelfth brightest star.
